Amorphous Germanium

With the vastly different structures observed in amorphous systems from metallic glasses to silica to polymers, researchers have consistently turned to amorphous silicon (a-Si) and amorphous germanium (a-Ge) as the archetypical amorphous system. Despite the many models for a-Si and a-Ge, a description for the observed undercoordination and signature features present in high resolution pair correlation functions (PCFs) is yet to be found. a-Ge had an experimental coordination number of ~3.65 implying a significant portion of the germanium atoms weren't fully bonded. Our results were recently published with LAMs giving the first look at the undercoordinated structure. The LAMs reveal the undercooridanted Ge atoms adopting a buckled, germanene-like structure.
