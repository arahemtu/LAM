About Us

Al Rahemtulla

Ph.D. Candidate at University of Guelph

arahemtu@uoguelph.ca

B.Sc. Honours Physics, M.Sc. (University of Guelph)

 

In my master's research I worked with Stefan and Bruno on a collaborative project trying to understand the structural details of amorhpous germanium. Our combined efforts resulted in the creation of the LAM method. My work today expands on using LAMs to probe amorhpous structure further.

Dr. Stefan Kycia

Associatate Professor at University of Guelph

skycia@uoguelph.ca

B.Sc. (McGill University), M.Sc. (Univ. of Pennsylvania), Ph.D. (Iowa State University)

 

My research focus is on using x-ray diffraction in novel ways to solve difficult structure problems. This includes optimizing and developing my own beamline at the Canadaian Light Source, creating high resolution near field detectors for 3DXRD and of course resolving our high quality data though LAMs.

Dr. Bruno Tomberli

Physics Convenor at Capilano University

brunotomberli@capilanou.ca

B.Sc. (Univ of Winnipeg), M.Sc., Ph.D. (University of Guelph)

 

My adjunct status at University of Guelph allow me to conduct research work towards the goal of an atomic level understanding of the microscopic interactions in soft materials. At this time my work is primarily focused in two areas:  visualization of structures in amorphous silicon and membrane biophysics.
