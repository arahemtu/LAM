So what's this all about?

Local Atomic Motifs is a new means of interpreting atomic configurations through simulations. It was first conceptualized as a means of understanding the structure of undercoordinated amorphous germanium. The goal was to resolve how undercoordination managed to manifest. Though atomic models have been published with undercoordination, there was no answer to the specific strucutre and thus the LAM method was born.

In essence, the LAM method works by overlaying local clusters of atoms on top of one another and using consistent rotations to rigidly align atoms in the most ordered way. These rotations aim for simplicity so large sets of data can computed and visualized using readily available computing power.
